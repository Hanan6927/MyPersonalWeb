Who Am I?

I'm a Software engineering student, beginner freelancer at different websites, website developer using HTML, CSS and Bootstrap.

In 2018 summer which was after I took higher education examination, I joined University of Gondar(UoG) Girls Can Code(GCC) program which were held for about 3 months.

I can say that Girls Can Code program has changed mind totally. After that program I become intersted in tech related fields and began to explore more about software enginnering.

Let's see what I have learnt and what was the final project we have done.

Girls Can Code

About UoG GCC

It is a summer programs for girls who are from 9 up to 12 grades. It's aim is to impower girls in the involvelment of tech industry and that girls can do codes or even better than boys.😉

It consistes 35 students after an exam conducted to reduce the number of students applying to the program due to shortage of resources.

My Journey

Although I spent less time there, I am talking you this much is beacuse it totally changed my life up-side-down. I passed the exam conducted and be part of the students who are taking the program.

At first I was a little bit confused on what the instructors were saying, beacuse I never had any background on programming or coding. But mean while I became more interested in the field and enjoying the classes. Beside the coding part GCC was also giving a life skill traning every friday afternoon by inviting famous persons in tech industry, this builds me a lot in enhancing my self-confidence.

Final project

After compeleting the program or the courses, we were supposed to do a project by selecting a title which may solve the society's problem and grouped in teams. Our group select a title of Online Election(voting) System.

We worked hardly with in the given deadline. We used HTML5, CSS3 and PHP for the website, also in two languages which are amharic and english. After finishing the projects we have done a defence infront of well known and skill-full instructors.

Graduation

After the defence we knew that my team has won the final project.YAY 🤩 It was one of my unforgottable moments. we asked for the same presentation for the guests coming to graduation day and we did that.

We took our certificate of completion in the graduation day and a suprize had come that day. I have been selected to be gifted a laptop from University of Gondar. This was a short overview about my journey in GCC.

Ideas I live by?

1. The 1% Rule. To get really good at any skill or discipline, put yourself on a schedule and aim to get 1% better at some aspect of that skill with each new project. Over time, you’ll see your skills grow rapidly.

2. Be a Solution Finder. One of the most valuable skills in today’s economy is the ability to independently find solutions for tough problems. Our easy access to search engines and digital assistants makes it so easy to get in the habit of giving up when a solution isn’t immediately present, and I try my best to avoid developing this habit.

3. Ego hinders growth. I try to view being wrong as a good thing. Though having my views challenged will always cause my emotional side to go on the defensive at first, the best possible response afterward is to let that guard down and ask myself, “Could I be wrong here?” Often, I am.
